Houston Public Library

In this design for a branch of the Houston public library, we see a project fixated on the language and power of the facade. Drawing from the local vernacular of developer townhomes, this project reflects on the possibilities of a hyper-vernacular aesthetic that blends in yet entirely sticks out.

The project is broken down into separate developer styles. These styles are defined by the flattening, commodified language often associated with real estate. Styles like “Modern”, “European”, “Rustic”, and “Spanish” carry with them distinct baggage, with varying degrees of separation from any source material.
