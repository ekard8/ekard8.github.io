John Lewis Center for Social Justice

The John Lewis Center for Social Justice was my partner’s and my proposal for a community space located in the heart of Atlanta, Georgia’s Sweet Auburn district. Primarily, the project was interested in exploring the ability of architecture to memorialize and make concrete normally abstract concepts like collective memory. Every aspect of the building was designed to maximize the ability for “good trouble.” As a memorial to one of the United States’ greatest civil rights figures, this building had an incredible legacy to live up to. My partner and I attempted to do so by creating a building that could only exist in response to the life of Representative John Lewis.
